Caesar Cipher

What is it?

Caesar Cipher is a simple way to hide messages. It was used by Julius Caesar long ago to send secret military messages.

How does it work?

You move each letter in the alphabet by a fixed number. Like if you move by 3, A becomes D, B becomes E, etc. The process wraps around from Z to A.

Example:

HELLO with shift 3 becomes KHOOR

Additional Facts:

It's very easy to break if you know the pattern. There are only 25 possible shifts to try!

Today it's mainly used for learning about cryptography and in simple puzzles.

The famous ROT13 is a Caesar cipher with shift 13, often used in online forums.

Julius Caesar actually used a shift of 3 for most of his secret communications.

It was once called 'the unbreakable cipher' but can be cracked with modern techniques.

It's more secure than Caesar cipher but still not safe for real secrets today.

The longer the keyword, the stronger the encryption becomes.

It remained unbroken until Friedrich Kasiski developed a method to crack it in 1863.
